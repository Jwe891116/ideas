Wonder Woman
Competition

Coast Guard Team Alpha Crowned Wonder Woman 2025 Champions


In a most anxious, high energy and combative Wonder Woman Competition yet your Coast Guard TEAM ALPHA was crowned 2025 Champions. In its nine years of running never have we witnessed such a tightly contested competition where the lead was alternated after every event culminating on the final event, the Crucible. Initiating six events in three days our women across the Tri-service certainly demonstrated a high level of physical fitness, determination, leadership and team spirit that inspired our nation. The Wonder Woman Competition is evidently the most arduous challenge in our Country and our women conquered it in competitive fashion and high spirits. The BDF and Coast Guard teams were equally matched. alternating lead throughout and winning by seconds on occasions leaving fans and supporters on edge and engaged throughout the three days of activities. Team Police had a special drive and motivation that brought continuous energy throughout the competition. They were always on the edge, on the verge of winning this one, or the next event. And they kept that motivation throughout. At the start of the competition Team BDF demonstrated the best total body fitness in the Coast Guard Fitness Test that comprised push-ups, sit ups, pull ups and a two-mile run. Lance Corporal Paquit knocked out at impressive 76 push ups in the two-minute drill. And whilst the Coast Guard team had the best running time, the overall tally placed the defending champs BDF in the lead. The lead changed after the one-mile swim – two mile run in light chop to choppy seas, an event that favored the Coast Guard team due to the swim. Coast Guard Seaman Chrysta Guydis had the best swimming time with forty-nine minutes. At the end of day one The BDF and Coast Guard were tied. Day two started with a six mile road run that was won by the Coast Guard by a mere 17 seconds. However the BDF tied the competition at the end of day two by winning the Medley by 20 seconds. Day three started with a march and shoot competition through the Hattieville – Boom road culminating at the Hattieville gallery range. BDF won the 8 mile road march putting pressure on the Coast Guard on the shooting event. Both BDF and Coast Guard fell all twenty falling plates, but the Coast Guard did it in a better time and also handed in 17 live rounds less than the BDF resulting in a tie after the march and shoot. Team Police also had a very good shooting day by hitting 11 of the falling plates.

Coast Guard Rescues Man Drifting on Makeshift Raft South of Belize City


A man was rescued by the Belize Coast Guard after he was lost at sea for several hours. On March 25th, around 5:00 p.m., the Belize Coast Guard received information of a man drifting on a makeshift raft approximately 17 miles south of Belize City. The BCG dispatched a crew and found 57-year-old Carlos Pinto sitting on a bucket while drifting on the raft made of wood and metal. Pinto was reportedly confused and could not provide the seamen with his name or address, leading to the BCG pleading for the public’s assistance on Facebook. The post was seen by his family, and he was identified. Pinto reportedly grew weary of working as a security guard on an island outside Belize City and decided to set off. He was found with his personal belongings, food, and a wooden paddle. Commandant of the Belize Coast Guard, Rear Admiral Elton Bennett, spoke about the operation. After the rescue, Pinto was transported to the Karl Heusner Memorial Hospital for medical attention.

Salary Increase!

Belize Coast Guard Working to Increase Salaries for Hundreds of Personnel Under "People's Operation"


The Belize Coast Guard is working to raise the salaries of its members who are being underpaid. Coast Guard Commandant Rear Admiral Elton Bennett explained that the BCG launched the People's Operation, last year, an initiative aimed at reviewing salaries and getting personnel properly compensated. Bennett says that the need for the operation was brought up at the last BCG senior officers conference. Since the operation has rolled out, around 200 personnel have gotten a salary increase with more on the way.

Stolen Vessel
Ring!

Stolen Vessels Ring Linked to Honduras: Coast Guard Tackles Ongoing Issue


The issue of stolen vessels is also one that the Belize Coast Guard has had to tackle in the past year. Commander Elton Bennett told Love News that there is a ring that extends as far as Honduras when it comes to stolen vessels in Belize's waters.

RADM Elton Bennett, Belize Coast Guard Commandant:
"And as we relate to maritime crime, there's a lot of vessel theft as of recent targeting areas of San Pedro, Caye Caulker and even down in Placencia that we are very concerned about. So again, this is a new phenomena that is plaguing our maritime space. We believe that there is a ring between Belize and Honduras where these vessels would be stripped for engine and other accessories."

Stays Alert!

Belize Coast Guard Remains on Alert for Holiday Maritime Security


Law enforcement officials will be on high alert over the Christmas season on both land and sea. As it relates to maritime security, Rear Admiral Elton Bennett of the Belize Coast Guard told Love News that his teams will be deployed at all bases. He noted that the holiday strategy also entails having support teams on call to assist should any situation develop. As families make final preparations for the Christmas, Commander Bennett, expressed gratitude to those who support the seamen and officers on call for the holidays.

RADM Elton Bennett, Belize Coast Guard Commandant:
"The Coast Guard remains operationally committed throughout the holiday season. We have our Forward Operating Basses that are up and operational. We have aggressive patrolling in areas where we believe there may be vulnerable mariners and fisherfolk so we have a specially designed operation to ensure that both maritime security but also maritime safety of mariners transiting Belizean waters is kept on our Coast Guard radar and that we're able to respond to any eventualities that may occur out at sea during this period. So as far north as Consejo our border with Mexico and as far south as Barranco and Hunting Caye the Belize Coast Guard will continue to patrol those areas maintaining watch across our sea spaces, throughout the Christmas season. We do not collapse operations at any point. Our operations remain forward deployed. The state of readiness and the ability to respond to any maritime eventualities is still out there. There's no reconfiguring of operations to scale it down. As a matter of fact over this Christmas season, over the holidays we remain an extra level of vigilance because people become vulnerable during this time of year as well and that translates to mariners out at sea as well. So the Coast Guard maintains watch."

Protection

Coast Guard Launches Marine Conservation Initiative


The Belize Coast Guard has partnered with the Department of the Environment to launch "Operation Blue Shield," a new initiative to protect marine ecosystems and combat illegal fishing activities. The program will deploy specialized patrol units to monitor protected areas, including the Belize Barrier Reef Reserve System. In its first month of operation, the initiative has already resulted in the interception of three vessels engaged in illegal fishing activities and the documentation of several cases of marine habitat destruction. The Coast Guard has committed to dedicating 20% of its operational resources to environmental protection missions moving forward.
