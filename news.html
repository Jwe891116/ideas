Wonder Woman Competition

Day 2 of the Wonder Woman Competition saw the Belize Coast Guard and the Belize Defence Force pull even at 380 points each heading into the final day, while the Belize Police Department trailed at 320 points. The day’s events featured a grueling six-mile run and the CG Medley, both testing the endurance and resilience of the competitors. Love News spoke with Major Roberta Usher of the Belize Defence Force, who shared her thoughts on the day’s hard-fought victory and what it meant for her team.

Major Robertha Usher, Belize Defense Force: “ Every day it gets harder and harder because the body is worn by now but it was alright. You train for it. This morning we were a little disappointed because losing a run by seconds is demoralizing. We just kind of pushed through this afternoon. It was a close call. I don’t even think the official time is out yet. But I think we made it. From the get-go I told them, I said after every event we just erase it and start new. It’s a new ball game and just everybody has to remember everybody is tired. We’re all giving our effort, so even if you feel like you could do a little bit more, you push yourself. It doesn’t matter what somebody else is doing, because at the end of the day, it’s one team.” The competition has seen the Belize Police Department face challenges, but this has not dampened the morale of its participants. Assistant Commissioner of Police, Jane Usher, emphasized that, regardless of the outcome, they take pride in knowing they have given their absolute best.

Jane Usher, Assistant Superintendent of Police: “My team has been sticking together and I’m really proud of the girls for that. You know, we know, like I said, we know our strengths, we know our weaknesses, we know what is the best we can do and at the end of the day, all we can do is give it our all. And I think we did that, especially on this last run, even on the six mile run this morning. This morning we had a six mile run. In training, our best time as a team was one hour. We did a little bit more than that, but that’s after two events yesterday. So the girls… pushed and that’s all I ask of them. This afternoon I think we really did well. I wasn’t supposed to even compete so just jumping in to assist them so that we could have a stronger team tomorrow as well we’re just trying to strategize as best we can and learning as we go and I think the girls are really doing their best so that’s good for us. We’re a bit behind in points and you know but we’re going to give it our all and even if we win one event or you know one evolution as they call it we get excited for that because we know at the end of the day, even if we get third we put in the best that we could. And so it doesn’t matter when we line up, we go in believing that we can do this. We are females just like they are. We are law enforcement just like they are. We put on our socks one foot at a time, just like they do. And so we go believing that we can do it and then may the best team win.”

The final events will be the March and Shoot and the CG Crucible.

Coast Guard Rescues

Yesterday, a man was rescued by the Belize Coast Guard after he was lost at sea for several hours. Around 5:00 p.m., the Belize Coast Guard received information of a man drifting on a makeshift raft approximately 17 miles south of Belize City. The BCG dispatched a crew and found 57-year-old Carlos Pinto sitting on a bucket while drifting on the raft made of wood and metal. Pinto was reportedly confused and could not provide the seamen with his name or address, leading to the BCG pleading for the public’s assistance on Facebook. The post was seen by his family, and he was identified. Pinto reportedly grew weary of working as a security guard on an island outside Belize City and decided to set off. He was found with his personal belongings, food, and a wooden paddle. Commandant of the Belize Coast Guard, Rear Admiral Elton Bennett, spoke about the operation.

Salary Increase

The Belize Coast Guard is working to raise the salaries of its members who are being underpaid. Coast Guard Commandant Rear Admiral Elton Bennett explained that the BCG launched the People’s Operation, last year, an initiative aimed at reviewing salaries and getting personnel properly compensated. Bennett says that the need for the operation was brought up at the last BCG senior officers conference. Since the operation has rolled out, around 200 personnel have gotten a salary increase with more on the way.

Stolen Vessel Ring

The issue of stolen vessels is also one that the Belize Coast Guard has had to tackle in the past year. Commander Elton Bennett told Love News that there is a ring that extends as far as Honduras when it comes to stolen vessels in Belize’s waters.

RADM Elton Bennett, Belize Coast Guard Commandant: “And as we relate to maritime crime, there’s a lot of vessel theft as of recent targeting areas of San Pedro, Caye Caulker and even down in Placencia that we are very concerned about. So again, this is a new phenomena that is plaguing our maritime space. We believe that there is a ring between Belize and Honduras where these vessels would be stripped for engine and other accessories.”

Holiday Alert

Law enforcement officials will be on high alert over the Christmas season on both land and sea. As it relates to maritime security, Rear Admiral Elton Bennett of the Belize Coast Guard told Love News that his teams will be deployed at all bases. He noted that the holiday strategy also entails having support teams on call to assist should any situation develop. As families make final preparations for the Christmas, Commander Bennett, expressed gratitude to those who support the seamen and officers on call for the holidays.

RADM Elton Bennett, Belize Coast Guard Commandant: “The Coast Guard remains operationally committed throughout the holiday season. We have our Forward Operating Basses that are up and operational. We have aggressive patrolling in areas where we believe there may be vulnerable mariners and fisherfolk so we have a specially designed operation to ensure that both maritime security but also maritime safety of mariners transiting Belizean waters is kept on our Coast Guard radar and that we’re able to respond to any eventualities that may occur out at sea during this period. So as far north as Consejo our border with Mexico and as far south as Barranco and Hunting Caye the Belize Coast Guard will continue to patrol those areas maintaining watch across our sea spaces, throughout the Christmas season. We do not collapse operations at any point. Our operations remain forward deployed. The state of readiness and the ability to respond to any maritime eventualities is still out there. There’s no reconfiguring of operations to scale it down. As a matter of fact over this Christmas season, over the holidays we remain an extra level of vigilance because people become vulnerable during this time of year as well and that translates to mariners out at sea as well. So the Coast Guard maintains watch.”

Environmental Protection

The Belize Coast Guard has launched a new environmental protection initiative focusing on marine conservation. This program includes regular patrols of protected areas, monitoring of fishing activities, and collaboration with environmental organizations to preserve Belize's marine ecosystems for future generations.
